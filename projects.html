WikiFreaks (in progress)

Course: LIS 546, Data Curation II, Spring 2023

Co-Creator: Lily Woodard

WikiFreaks is an in-progress repository of datasets of yearly Top 25 Reports on the English-speaking Wikipedia and the categories each page in the report belongs to, as well as documentation and guidelines for uploading and downloading.

Technologies Used: GitHub/GitBook, XML, Markdown, OpenRefine

Deliverables:

  • Statement of Work
  • User Stories
  • Collection Policy

Baloney Detectives: A Virtual Misinformation Escape Room

Course: LIS 547, Design Methods, Winter 2023

Co-Creators: Andrew Charlton-Jones, Shira Gottfried, and Sabrina Hsu.

Baloney Detectives is a virtual, text-based escape room modeled after Choose-Your-Own-Adventure (CYOA) games to build resilience towards misinformation in a fun and engaging manner. The game features four puzzles surrounding different kinds of misinformation on social media and the Internet, reflecting what teenage and young adult users may encounter in their digital landscapes.

We later had the opportunity to present this at the UW Center for an Informed Public's annual MisInfo Day conference, after our professor recommended it to the CIP faculty and staff.

Technologies Used: Twine, namely HTML and CSS through the platform (for game design), itch.io (for hosting)

Deliverables:

  • MisInfo Day Presentation and Demo
  • Final Design Document

Re-Balancing Catan with Value-Sensitive Design

Course: LIS 547, Design Methods, Winter 2023

This was a writing assignment I turned into a public blog post on my instructor's recommendation. I used value-sensitive design methods to make the classic board game Catan more accessible to new players while keeping the interests of more experienced players in mind.

This felt like a tiny passion project, as I was able to implement systems analysis and design into a personal hobby of mine, and set the tone for many things I would explore later in the MLIS program and professionally.

Technologies Used: Figma (for prototyping), Medium (for writing, editing, and publishing)

GSRI 2023 Website (in progress)

Position: Graduate Assistant, UW Libraries Instructional Design

Co-Creators: Perry Yee (project supervisor), Robin Chin-Roemer (unit supervisor), Amber Mak

The Graduate Student Research Institute (GSRI) is a yearly multi-day online workshop offered by the LibID team to help incoming graduate students across disciplines gain a better understanding of research topics and connect with one another before their programs begin.

Based on a review of survey data that Amber (the other GA) and I led, we decided to change the format and offer it publicly over Omeka rather than privately on Canvas, and transferred the content to a static site on there through UW's server.

This is a big improvement in GSRI's overall accessibility, since the pages can be shorter and the Canvas format caused participants to be confused at times. We are set to deploy this in the summer, once we create some video content and plan the synchronous (Zoom) sessions which students can opt into.

Technologies Used: Omeka
