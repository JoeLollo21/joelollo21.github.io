Four Climate Corners

Sample Lesson: Understanding Climate Emotions

This lesson gets participants moving around the room to introduce concepts like eco-grief, climate anxiety and fear, and other ecological emotions. By moving to points that align with one’s emotional state, different affects are made visible within a group gathering.

The activity is adapted from the book Active Hope by Joanna Macy.

Circles of Support

Sample Lesson: Identifying Support in Environmental Crises

In his book Transformational Resilience, psychiatrist Bob Doppelt explains that “in the midst of trauma and stress, it is our family, friends, neighbors, and other members of our personal social support network who are most likely to provide the practical assistance and emotional sustenance needed to stabilize our nervous system.”

This sample lesson helps individuals identify personal strengths, skills, resources, and social networks available to them, which are vital to staying calm and centered in the midst of ecological adversity.
